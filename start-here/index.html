A gentle guide to big feelings

Parenting little kids with big feelings can feel overwhelming, lonely, and confusing — especially when tantrums, anger, or emotional outbursts show up again and again.

This space is where I share real moments from our home — the struggles, the growth, the mistakes, and the small wins — in hopes that it helps you feel less alone and more confident in your own parenting journey.

If you’re new here, these five posts are the best place to begin.

“I Hate You! I Wish You Weren’t My Mom!”

A raw, honest moment many parents are afraid to admit out loud. This post explores what it feels like to hear those words — and how love, boundaries, and emotional safety can coexist.

Rules of Anger

When my son wrote his own “rules” for anger, it changed everything. This post shares how children can find their own paths to regulation — if we give them the space and trust to do so.

Taming Tantrums

A calm moment in the middle of a storm. This story shows how modeling regulation can be more powerful than lectures, consequences, or raised voices.

Moments of Weakness

Parenting isn’t about getting it right all the time. This post shares a moment I’m not proud of — and what it taught me about shame, expectations, and self-compassion.

Being a Kid Can Really Stink

Sometimes kids don’t have the words — but books help. This gentle story shows how children express big feelings when we truly listen.

 

If these stories resonate with you, you may also enjoy our picture books about emotions — created to help children name, accept, and work through big feelings in a safe, age-appropriate way.
