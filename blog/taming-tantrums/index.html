Taming Tantrums: A Small Moment That Made a Big Difference

Kim T. S. December 8, 2021

Today, I want to share a proud moment of strength.

A few months ago, we had reached a really good place—tantrums had become rare, and things felt manageable.

When school routines shifted again, all of that seemed to fall apart. We had our fair share of moments we weren’t proud of. I’ll save those for another day.

For now, let’s focus on strength.

❤

April 20, 2021

I gave Kyle a heads-up that he only had a few minutes left on his iPad. When the timer rang, I took it and said,

“Time’s up.”

Usually, he relents. This time, he didn’t.

He started whining and shouting, “NO!” Then my phone rang. I answered it — and that pushed him over the edge.

He began hitting me softly. As I reached to put the iPad out of reach, he kicked me.

I looked him in the eye and said,

“Stop it.”

It worked — for about a second.

After I finished my call, I squatted down and tried to help him regulate.

“Do you need a hug?”

No.

“Water?”

No.

“Should you run to your bed to keep yourself from hitting, like you said you would last time?”

No.

“Take a deep breath?”

No.

“Think of something funny? Hug your monkey?”

No. No. No.

He looked me straight in the eye while hitting and kicking me, and I could feel myself nearing my own breaking point.

So I stopped.

I closed my eyes and took deep breaths. Then I said out loud:

“I am getting upset. But even if Kyle hits me, he doesn’t mean it. I know that he loves me. And I love him.”

Suddenly, he wrapped his arms around me.

He hugged me tightly. I felt his breathing slow.

Softly, he repeated my words back to me:

“Mommy loves me. And I love my mommy.”

I hugged him back — and I felt his anger melt away.

❤

“What should we do now, Mom?” he asked. “What do you feel like doing?”

“Should we read some books?”

“Okay!” he said. “I really wanted to read Llama Llama last night.”

We read together. Afterward, we talked about what had happened.

He chuckled and said,

“I got so angry, Mom.”

He apologized. He admitted he was wrong. He promised to try my strategy next time — because it really, really worked.

Spoiler alert:

It didn’t work the next time.

😆

But that’s life. We just keep trying.

💙

If this moment resonated with you, feel free to share it with someone who might need it.
