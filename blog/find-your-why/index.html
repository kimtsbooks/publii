Finding Your Why: Work, Parenting, and Big Feelings

Kim T. S. November 23, 2021

Find your why.

What inspires you to keep going—especially on the days when work feels heavy or parenting feels overwhelming?

For us, our why comes from a simple hope: that children who read our books feel comforted knowing they are not alone in their big feelings. We hope parents feel encouraged to embrace the emotional ups and downs of raising children—and to support kids in expressing themselves in healthy, compassionate ways.

We hope more children grow up to become emotionally regulated adults. And in their own quiet, everyday moments, we hope they help make the world a little kinder.

And if somewhere out there, a struggling adult also finds comfort in our books—whether as a parent, a teacher, or simply as someone who needed reassurance—that would mean more to us than we can put into words.

Finding your why doesn’t always mean loving every part of what you do. Sometimes your work feels deeply fulfilling. Other times, it’s simply what allows you to support yourself or your family. And sometimes, the most meaningful work happens at home—raising children, showing up, and holding space for big emotions.

All of it matters.

On the days when it feels hard to get through another week, we hope you can reconnect with your why—even if it feels small or quiet—and let it be enough to help you keep moving forward.

 

If this resonated with you, you may find our books helpful as gentle tools for navigating fear, worry, frustration, and other big emotions together.
