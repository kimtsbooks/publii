Our Favorite Books About Feelings

As much as we love writing books about feelings, we know there are many wonderful stories out there that explore emotions in meaningful ways too.

Every author brings a unique voice, style, and message—and every family connects with different books. You don’t have to choose just one.

Here are some of our favorite books about feelings so far, in no particular order. We’ll keep adding to this list as we discover more.

“It’s okay to feel all your feelings — you can’t always help how you feel. It's important to remember these feelings are normal and real.”
— Feeling All My Feelings, by Kim T. S.
 

Hello Genius Books — Michael Dahl

We fell in love with these books when Kyle was still a toddler. They’re simple, relatable, and perfect for very young children.

Little Monkey Calms Down helped Kyle through several intense moments during his “terrible twos.”

Now that he’s older, he gravitates toward more challenging reads — but these books supported us through many early milestones. For that, we’ll always be grateful.

 

A Little SPOT Series — Diane Alber

We discovered this series through Kindle Unlimited, and Kyle immediately wanted to download more.

We read several together before he moved on to other books, but the message and illustrations really stood out.

In our experience, this series may be better suited for slightly older kids, but the concepts are thoughtful and presented in a gentle, accessible way.

 

The Feelings Book & The Goodbye Book — Todd Parr

Kyle’s grandpa passed away when Kyle was three. A close friend sent us The Goodbye Book, and it brought us comfort during a very tender time.

Todd Parr has a gift for presenting big, difficult ideas with simplicity, warmth, and honesty — in a way children can truly understand.

 

Feeling All My Feelings — Kim T. S.

Last—our own Feeling All My Feelings collection.

These books grew out of our experiences navigating big emotions at home. They’re written in rhyme, designed to be read together, and meant to help children name, accept, and move through their feelings—without judgment.

If you’re looking for books that support emotional awareness for both kids and adults, we hope you’ll find them helpful.
