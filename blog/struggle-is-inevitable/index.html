Struggle is Inevitable

Kim T. S. August 12, 2024

I went back to full-time work after a four-year sabbatical. While it was exciting, I deeply missed being at home with my son. I started second-guessing myself and wondering if I had made the right decision.

Eventually, I found my rhythm again and settled into work. I wrote the words below in my journal a few months ago and completely forgot about them—until I reread them recently. They resonated with me, and I felt called to share them.

If you ever find yourself sitting with similar doubts, I hope this helps you through it.

Struggle is inevitable.

When it shows up again, draw confidence from your past experiences. You have struggled before. You doubted yourself—yet you overcame those moments and came out stronger and wiser.

Maybe self-doubt is a feeling you’re meant to sit with for a while.

You can listen to it speak. Understand where it’s coming from. Then, gently and respectfully, disagree with it.

You may feel a lack of confidence—but you can choose to believe something else: that despite how it feels right now, you’ve already proven, time and again, that you’re capable of more than you give yourself credit for.

Maybe these low moments are necessary for growth. They encourage reflection and protect us from the bigger mistakes that can come from unchecked confidence.

Accept that this feeling will visit from time to time. Welcome it. Learn from it. And remember—it is temporary.

Find the lesson, so you can let it go.

You are not your worst enemy—you are your best challenger. You challenge yourself to keep growing, even when it’s hard. And look how far you’ve already come because of it.

Believe in yourself.

 

These reflections often shape the stories I write for children, too.

If this resonates with you, you’re welcome to send me a note. I’d truly love to hear your story.
